① Spacetime Grid

The universe has a 4D fabric. A flat grid shows no gravity — objects move in straight lines. Here the grid is distorted by the sun.

② Gravity = Geometry

Mass tells spacetime how to curve. The steeper the warp, the stronger the pull. Earth's orbit is a straight line through curved space.

③ Light Bends Too

Even light follows the curve. Near massive objects, starlight bends — this is how we discover hidden black holes and weigh stars.
